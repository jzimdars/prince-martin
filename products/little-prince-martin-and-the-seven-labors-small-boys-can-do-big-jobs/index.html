Little Prince Martin and the Seven Labors: Small Boys Can Do Big Jobs

On his birthday, a boy Prince discovers he’s old enough to lend a hand around the castle and help his neighbors. And he gets some wonderful presents along the way.

Little boys who love big adventure will adore this heartwarming tale with gorgeous full-color art and playful rhyming verse.

And parents will appreciate the subtle morals the story imparts.

This uplifting picture book is a prequel to the popular Prince Martin Epic chapter book series.

Printed with premium color and premium paper.

8.5″ x 8.5″, 34 pages. Full color.

Available in: Paperback, Hardcover, and Kindle

Is your son a reluctant reader?

The Prince Martin Epic series is a perfect fit because each book features:

  • High-stakes action-adventure and danger
  • Brilliant color illustrations that burst off the page
  • Text that appears to be prose—but subtly rhymes, gently nudging the boy along as he is encouraged to find the next rhyme

This is the perfect formula for engaging boy readers.

The Prince Martin Epic series is adventure books that fill a need: because they RHYME your son will read!

This book series will hook your son on reading because the subtly rhyming text fascinates reluctant readers, who can’t wait to discover the next epic rhyme. Your son will also love the books because of the explosive art and high-stakes action-adventure. These are not “safe” books!

Parents will appreciate these classic tales because they build character, model virtue, and inspire grit and resilience in boys. The series grows with your son: Each successive book gets longer and the plot more action-packed! And each book can be read as a stand-alone.

Introduce your son to Prince Martin today because boys need virtuous heroes!

Short but Satisfying - Great Book for Boys

I have read with enjoyment every book in the world of Prince Martin, and this short storybook does not disappoint. The seven books in the Epic gradually grow in length and depth. This is a prequel, a good introduction to our young hero, and a stand alone story book that both pleases and teaches. I was surprised by the meatiness of the vocabulary. The five to eight year old target audience will need their parents’ help with “denote”, “facile” and “auspicious”. I had to look up “shoat” myself. But the story is very understandable, and relatable to the lads it’s aimed at. On his birthday, the Prince is sent out to perform seven useful tasks - and he gets rewarded with a practical gift each time. More importantly, he is given instruction on how to use his rewards, and he learns that work can be fun, that he should do things without being told - and that charity to the disadvantaged is blessed. The plot is clear and resolves itself by the end. I highly recommend this book about a princeling’s birthday. It would make a good gift for any lad; even better, make it a birthday adventure by tasking your little squire with missions that earn the same or similar rewards. Buy this book for your laddie. When he likes it, give him the seven books in the epic, which will grow with him. (There is a coloring book too.) Full disclosure - as a Prince Martin aficionado, the author advanced me the Kindle edition for review. But I have already ordered the paperback. Old fashioned adventurers prefer libraries to ephemeral phantom formats. And, btw, a “shoat” is a piglet just weaned. If I ever knew that, I had forgotten it.
Scott R.
