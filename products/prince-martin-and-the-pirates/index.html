Prince Martin and the Pirates

When Prince Martin is captured by the Dread Pirate Kaine and his perfidious crew, the kid discovers that his only hope might come from the sea itself...

In this epic illustrated adventure told in riveting rhyming verse, Prince Martin, his Band of Brothers, and his father—the King—embark on a dangerous quest to recover friends lost at sea. Their exciting voyage takes them to the Tropical Main, where unfathomable secrets are concealed in the deep. But that once-peaceful sea is now plagued by the Dread Pirate Kaine, his gruesome pets, and his infamous crew. When these notorious sea rovers capture the boy prince, Martin discovers that his only hope might come from the sea itself...

Read this action-packed saga as a stand-alone story or as part of the Prince Martin Epic series.

Ages 10 and up. Read as a stand-alone or as Book 6 in the series.

Available in: Paperback, Hardcover, and Kindle. 9″ x 6″, 163 pages.

Is your son a reluctant reader?

The Prince Martin Epic series is a perfect fit because each book features:

  • High-stakes action-adventure and danger
  • Brilliant color illustrations that burst off the page
  • Text that appears to be prose—but subtly rhymes, gently nudging the boy along as he is encouraged to find the next rhyme

This is the perfect formula for engaging boy readers.

The Prince Martin Epic series is adventure books that fill a need: because they RHYME your son will read!

This book series will hook your son on reading because the subtly rhyming text fascinates reluctant readers, who can’t wait to discover the next epic rhyme. Your son will also love the books because of the explosive art and high-stakes action-adventure. These are not “safe” books!

Parents will appreciate these classic tales because they build character, model virtue, and inspire grit and resilience in boys. The series grows with your son: Each successive book gets longer and the plot more action-packed! And each book can be read as a stand-alone.

Introduce your son to Prince Martin today because boys need virtuous heroes!

Can Prince Martin and his Band defeat a monstrous pirate and his crew?

A merchant ship is boarded by a black galleon full of pirates in the Tropical Main. But this was no ordinary assault, the Pirate Captain had a bird on his shoulder with a metal beak, and this vulture spoke instead of the captain and threatened the ships crew with death if they tried to escape, and the pirate captain looked like a ghost with greasy black hair, coal black eyes and he was 7 feet tall! The King, Queen, Prince Martin and his advisors held a meeting to discuss what had happened to one of the Kings ships out on the Coconut Sea. Ned, a sailor, told them about a wreck he had seen that had the Kings crest but she was full of holes, her mast was destroyed, and she was battered and burning and empty of cargo. A black pirate flag flew from her hull, this signified that pirates had destroyed one of the Kings’ ships, the white skull of a bird was on it signifying that Caddaric Kane destroyed her. Then Ned told an awful tale of joining these pirates as a young teenager, he told how the Pirate Captain had venomous hissing jailers that kept the prisoners working for them and even using them as food! Then Ned repented of his life at 16 and became a fisherman. The King forgave Ned and told him his new life absolved him Ned offered his assistance apprehending Captain Kane.
